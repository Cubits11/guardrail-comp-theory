\section{Causal Interaction and Reintroduction}
Reintroduction occurs when the deployment of rail $A$ changes the behaviour of rail $B$ on harmful
content: $\Prb(B=1\mid Y=1,A=0) \neq \Prb(B=1\mid Y=1)$. Under an assumption of conditional
independence $A \indep B \mid (X,Y)$ for latent features $X$, interventional and observational
quantities coincide, so $\Prb(B\mid Y,\mathrm{do}(A)) = \Prb(B\mid Y)$. A limited interventional budget
can detect violations via regression adjustment and hypothesis testing, supplying a practical check
before reintroducing filtered items.
